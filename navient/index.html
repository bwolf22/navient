The Battle of Student Loans


By: Ben Wolf


For the University of Maryland and Rutgers University, the battle of I-95 is an East Coast rivalry that has blossomed on the Football field. While the two universities battle over sports, they are also battling issues with student loans.

According to Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, in the past year, Maryland has received 371 complaints regarding student loans, while New Jersey has received 403. These complaints range from issues with loan repayment plans to difficulties in communicating with lenders.

Whether it be about how the payments are being handled or about not agreeing with the fees people are being charged, it is a prevalent issue in Big Ten states. In both states, over 80 percent of the loans students and families have had problems with have come from federal servicing.

Student loans have been controversial for many years, and improvements to the system are still needed. In the meantime, to avoid being taken advantage of, applicants must conduct extensive research before making a decision.
